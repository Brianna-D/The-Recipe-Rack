American Cheeseburger:

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef
  • 1/2 lb (225g) ground sirloin
  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) steak sauce
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 tomatoes
  • Red onion, thinly sliced
  • Dill pickle
  • Salt and pepper
  • 4 American cheese
  • 4 lettuce leaves
Instructions:
  1. Mix the ground meats, steak sauce, egg yolks, salt, and pepper
  2. Add a small pat of butter inside each patty before grilling
  3. Form the patties and grill until cooked through
  4. Top with American cheese, lettuce, tomato, onion, and pickles

Sushi

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (400g) sushi rice
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 8 oz (225g) sashimi-grade fish (tuna, salmon, etc.)
  • Wasabi paste
  • Soy sauce for serving

Instructions

  1. Cook sushi rice according to package instructions. Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small bowl
  2. Spread cooked rice in a large, flat container. Sprinkle vinegar mixture over rice and fold gently to combine. Let cool to room temperature
  3. Slice fish into thin pieces, about 2 inches long and 1 inch wide
  4. Wet your hands with water and vinegar mixture. Take about 2 tablespoons of rice and shape into an oval
  5. Place a small amount of wasabi on top of the rice
  6. Lay a slice of fish over the rice, pressing gently to adhere
  7. Using your thumb and forefinger, gently press the sides of the fish to curve it around the rice
  8. Serve with soysauce

Traditional Kenyan Mandazi:

Ingredients

  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup (100g) sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) coconut milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (plus more for frying)
  • Warm water as needed
  • Powdered sugar or chocolate for topping (optional)
Instructions
  1. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and cardamom.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together coconut milk, egg, and 2 tbsp vegetable oil.
  3. Add wet ingredients to dry ingredients, mixing to form a soft dough. Add warm water as needed if the dough is too dry.
  4. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until smooth. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  5. Roll out the dough to about 1/2 cm thickness. Cut into triangles or desired shapes.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C).
  7. Fry the mandazi in batches until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  8. Drain on paper towels and serve warm, optionally dusted with powdered sugar or topped with chocolate.
